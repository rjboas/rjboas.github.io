Bubble Tea #3

A retrospective review (i.e. I'm not sipping on one of these as I'm writing).
The tea: Taro Milk Tea
Quality varies between stores, but usually a solid choice with pearls or red bean (nice flavour, but my impression of the texture in a beverage is more mixed). Definitely a "less sugar" milk tea. I'm personally a fan of thicker beverages so that works for me also.
Overall: recommended.

Bubble Tea #2

Because everything is funnier the second time.
The tea: Honeydew Milk Tea with pearls
Refreshing flavour, but still with a nice milky texture. Less sugar is the way to go imo. Pearls work well, but I imagine other toppings would pair well too.
Overall: recommended (if you like honeydew).

Bubble Tea #1

An archive of a random "review" I sent to a group chat.
The tea: Thick Coconut Brulee Milk Tea
The combination of fresh milk and coconut flavour is quite strange, but I found mixing in the brulee cream made it smoother tasting. The chocolate biscuit crumbs work well to contrast. Coconut jelly is generally pretty good.
Overall: not strongly recommended. You could probably just try coconut jelly in fresh milk or milk tea, or spend the reasonable amount of money on something you prefer better.
